AFTERNOON LEAGUE

CODE OF CONDUCT WHEN PLAYING MATCHES


As last season progressed it became clear that our standards are slipping.
While we play this game for pleasure we still play competitively and we all try to win games and matches.
Nevertheless, we have a set of rules, as there is with any sport, competitive or not.
Such rules are put in place not to hinder taking part but so that there is a ‘level playing field’ and so that any one player does not have an advantage over any other.
Also if and when we then play in a higher level competition we don’t have to remember the true rules because we are already playing to them.
Below I have set out a list of aspects of the game where these principles seem to be slipping as well as some general points covering conduct, care of equipment and so on.

  1. Players must wear flat soled shoes
    This is to protect the surface of the mat and for the same reason it is unacceptable to wear the same shoes that you’ve worn outside. There are a number of acceptable alternatives to commercial bowling shoes – please check.

  2. Players must be aware of the foot fault rule.
    Check yourself before bowling (and also check other players).

  3. Players should be aware of the correct position for the fenders and the block.
    To make this easier clubs should install Velcro to the underside of the fenders so that once positioned they stay in place for the whole of the match.

  4. Players should ensure the foot mat is correctly positioned.
    Strictly, according to the rules, a foot fault can be called if a player bowls from an incorrectly positioned foot mat.

  5. Ensure measuring is carried out correctly.
    Although we’ve held one or two training sessions on this topic, some measuring is still done incorrectly. To be fair to all teams and players, captains must check that players doing the measuring are competent. If more training is required this can be arranged.

  6. Check the score with the other marker often
    suggest every two or three ends.

  7. In case of dispute consult the rule book.
    But also make use of experienced players, especially any qualified umpires, whichever team they represent.
