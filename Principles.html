BASIC PRINCIPLES FOR PLAYING MATCHES


The general principle in arranging games is that no player shall play more than one game against any opponent.

With nine pairs games in a match it is possible to have 18 players on each side but this would mean each player having only one game.

So it is a balance of the number of players and the number of games.

At present most captains chose one of the following set-ups:

  1. three pairs playing three times each or
  2. two pairs plus one set of three players, each playing twice or
  3. one pair plus two sets of three players, each playing twice or
  4. three sets of three players, each playing twice.

However, anything from these four usual arrangements to playing all 18 players conforms with the rules and it is entirely up to the captain as long as the general principle is applied.

In the event of the usual captain being away, it is most important to ensure that the person taking charge, arranging the pairing and filling in the result sheet knows how to do it. Please, go through the procedures thoroughly with your deputy.

A number of the result sheets are regularly filled in incorrectly. A specimen sheet showing what goes where has been circulated. Please make sure you understand the comments and ask if necessary.
