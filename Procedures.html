PROCEDURES FOR PAIRINGS AND PLAYING WITH RESERVES


  1. Basic format with 6 players is to form the pairs as A, B and C and NOT change throughout the match.

  2. With 7 players group them as follows:
    Pair A --- Player 1 / Player 2
    Pair B --- Player 3 / Player 4
    Pair C --- Player 5 / Player 6 / Player 7

    Pairs A and B then play unchanged throughout the match while Pair C rotate the players; that is Player 5 / Player 6 play the first game, Player 5 / Player 7 play the second game and Player 6 / Player 7 play the third game.

  3. With 8 players group them as follows:
    Pair A --- Player 1 / Player 2
    Pair B --- Player 3 / Player 4 / Player 5
    Pair C --- Player 6 / Player 7 / Player 8

    Then, similarly, Pair A plays unchanged and Pairs B and C rotate the players.

  4. With the same scheme even 9 players can be accommodated.

This scheme should ensure two things.

Firstly, all players have at least two games; the others obviously have three games.

It is up to the Captain to decide who plays the most.

Secondly, and most importantly, pairs from opposite teams should not play each other twice.
